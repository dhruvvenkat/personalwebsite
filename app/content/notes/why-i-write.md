I think writing is one of the most important skills a person can have right now. In a world where AI systems are getting better at interpreting natural language, the ability to think clearly in language becomes even more valuable. If prompts, specs, feedback, and plans increasingly shape software systems, then weak writing turns into weak leverage.

For me, writing is also a way to make ideas less vague. A thought can feel solid in your head until you try to put it into sentences. Then the holes show up. The assumptions show up. The fuzzy parts show up. That is useful. It is one of the fastest ways I know to sharpen judgment.

I usually keep things pretty technical online, but I want this section of the site to be broader than that. Some notes will be about engineering and AI tooling. Others will just be about what I am noticing, learning, or changing my mind on. The point is to document the process instead of pretending every thought has to arrive fully formed.
